# GDPR Key Articles — Reference

## Chapter 1: General Provisions (Art 1-4)
- **Art 1**: Subject matter and objectives
- **Art 2**: Material scope (household exemption, national security exclusion)
- **Art 3**: Territorial scope (establishment, targeting, monitoring)
- **Art 4**: Definitions (personal data, processing, controller, processor, consent)

## Chapter 2: Principles (Art 5-11)
- **Art 5**: Principles — lawfulness, fairness, transparency; purpose limitation; data minimisation; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity/confidentiality; accountability
- **Art 6**: Lawfulness of processing — 6 legal bases: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interest
- **Art 7**: Conditions for consent — demonstrable, withdrawable, unbundled
- **Art 8**: Child consent (digital services, age 16, or 13-15 with parental authorisation)
- **Art 9**: Special categories — prohibition + explicit consent or specific Derogation
- **Art 10**: Criminal convictions data
- **Art 11**: Processing not requiring identification

## Chapter 3: Data Subject Rights (Art 12-22)
- **Art 12**: Transparent communication — concise, transparent, intelligible, accessible; 1 month response
- **Art 13-14**: Information to be provided (collected from data subject vs elsewhere)
- **Art 15**: Right of access — confirmation, copy, processing info
- **Art 16**: Right to rectification
- **Art 17**: Right to erasure (right to be forgotten)
- **Art 18**: Right to restrict processing
- **Art 19**: Notification obligation regarding rectification/erasure/restriction
- **Art 20**: Right to data portability — structured, commonly used, machine-readable
- **Art 21**: Right to object — direct marketing (absolute), legitimate interest (rebuttable)
- **Art 22**: Automated individual decision-making — right to human intervention

## Chapter 4: Controller/Processor (Art 24-43)
- **Art 24**: Responsibility of the controller — implement appropriate measures
- **Art 25**: Data protection by design and by default
- **Art 26**: Joint controllers
- **Art 27**: Representatives of non-EU controllers
- **Art 28**: Processor — written contract, Art 28 conditions
- **Art 30**: Records of processing activities (ROPA)
- **Art 32**: Security of processing — appropriate technical/organisational measures
- **Art 33**: Breach notification to DPA (72h)
- **Art 34**: Breach communication to data subject
- **Art 35**: Data protection impact assessment (DPIA)
- **Art 36**: Prior consultation with DPA
- **Art 37**: Designation of DPO
- **Art 38**: Position of DPO — independent, no conflict of interest
- **Art 39**: Tasks of DPO

## Chapter 5: International Transfers (Art 44-49)
- **Art 44**: General principle — adequate level of protection required
- **Art 45**: Adequacy decisions
- **Art 46**: Appropriate safeguards (SCCs, BCRs, codes of conduct, certification)
- **Art 47**: Binding corporate rules (BCRs)
- **Art 48**: International cooperation / data subject rights
- **Art 49**: Derogations for specific situations (explicit consent, contract necessity, vital interests)

## Chapter 6: Supervisory Authorities (Art 50-59)
- **Art 51**: Establishment of DPA
- **Art 55**: Competence of lead DPA (one-stop-shop mechanism)
- **Art 56**: One-stop-shop — lead DPA for cross-border processing

## Chapter 7: Cooperation and Consistency (Art 60-76)
- **Art 60**: Cooperation between lead and concerned DPAs
- **Art 63**: Consistency mechanism
- **Art 64**: Opinion of EDPB on consistency matters

## Chapter 8: Remedies and Liability (Art 77-82)
- **Art 77**: Right to complain to DPA
- **Art 78**: Right to judicial remedy against DPA
- **Art 79**: Right to judicial remedy against controller/processor
- **Art 80**: Representation of data subjects (NGOs)
- **Art 82**: Right to compensation and liability

## Chapter 9: Specific Processing Situations (Art 85-91)
- **Art 85**: Processing and freedom of expression
- **Art 86**: Public access to official documents
- **Art 87**: National identification numbers
- **Art 88**: Employment context — Member State law
- **Art 89**: Archiving, research, statistical purposes — Derogations

## Chapter 10: Fines and Penalties (Art 83-84)
- **Art 83**: General conditions for fines — tiered: lower €10M/2%, upper €20M/4%
- **Art 84**: Penalties for non-material infringements (Member State law)

## Recitals (selected)
- **Rec 26**: Notions of anonymisation vs pseudonymisation
- **Rec 30**: Online identifiers (IP addresses, cookies)
- **Rec 47**: Legitimate interest — reasonable expectations, context of collection
- **Rec 51**: Special categories — protection against discrimination
- **Rec 65**: Right to erasure — children, social media
- **Rec 71**: Profiling — safeguards, appropriate mathematical/statistical procedures
- **Rec 75**: High risk — profiling, vulnerable persons, large-scale, monitoring
- **Rec 101**: International transfers — level of protection not undermined
